Explore the Influence Arcs

This diagram aligns each roguelike game by their release year, and draw influence arcs between the years. Thicker arcs depict documented and known influences. Thinner arcs are inferred influences gathered from web scraping, such as how often two games appear together in the same article.

Arcs above the horizon are influences between two roguelike games, and arcs below the horizon are influences between a roguelike and a non-roguelike game.

The Genre-Influence Matrix

The genre-influence matrix divides the diagram above into 4 quadrants. Each quadrant represents a pair made of in-genre/out-of-genre and past/future. This creates an interesting dynamic where a title can be representative of its own genre, taking most of its influence from the past, or influential by having many connections to its future, with respect to roguelike titles.

For instance, Recently published games may be representative of its genre now, but as time goes on, it may become more influential in the genre as other games follow its footsteps.

If we aggregate the number of influences for each game, we can plot them in a 2-dimensional map.

Laying out the Roguelike Influence Map

Similar to the diagram above, this map divides games into 4 quadrants. The position is calculated by adding up the number of influences in each quadrant. The further they are away from the origin, the more distinct their influences are. For instance, influences mostly in roguelike games will result in above the horizon line.

Click on a circle to see which games are at that location.

Roguelike (and Roguelike-like) Influence Timeline

Lastly, we produce a scatter plot of every roguelike, and 'roguelike-like' games. The horizontal axis is the release year (or the exact date as available), and the vertical, stacked axis is the number of influences each game has on other games. Games with no influences are omitted. Influences are drawn as links between the dots.

The primary aim of this chart is to not only show one 'hop' of influences, but also show multiple hops, influences of influences. Influences may form a small-world network.

Click on one of the known or inferred influences, and see where it leads you.

About this page

Roguelike Universe is an ongoing project to visualise roguelike influences. This project is seeking corrections, suggestions, and feedback.

Method

Known influence was collected through RogueTemple's IRLDB, with a hand-cleaned version stored online. You are welcome to edit the data. The search setting used can be found in this picture. Inferred influence was calculated by web scraping, gathering the first 10-30 links found via the DuckDuckGo search engine. Data was prepared in Python, with a little bit of post-processing in JavaScript in the browser. Figures are homemade and crafted in D3.js with care. We try to make the live computed dataset available, which can be downloaded below each figure.

If you decide to reference or cite this work, please cite the following paper: 

      Finding Design Influence within Roguelike Games (open access PDf)

Contribute

If you would like to contribute, the best way is to review and edit the wikipages at RogueBasin. In particular, if you are the author of the game, and you see errors in the dataset, go ahead and make the correction! We would love to hear about your changes.
